# Decision Rights Ladder

The decision rights ladder defines how much authority an agent can hold inside a workflow.

## Levels

1. Inform only
2. Draft only
3. Recommend options
4. Recommend one option
5. Execute after approval
6. Execute reversible actions
7. Execute bounded autonomous actions
8. Escalate exceptions automatically

## Assignment criteria

Use lower levels when decisions are ambiguous, high-risk, irreversible, or socially sensitive.

Use higher levels when work is repetitive, measurable, reversible, low-risk, and policy-bound.

## Review cadence

Decision rights should be reviewed after every pilot, incident, policy change, and major workflow redesign.
