# Legal and Compliance Department Redesign

## Current operating model

Legal and compliance teams review contracts, policies, risks, obligations, disputes, regulatory requirements, and business decisions.

## AI-native redesign

AI-native legal and compliance uses agents for intake triage, document comparison, clause retrieval, obligation tracking, policy support, and risk flagging while keeping legal judgment human-led.

## Human-led work

- Legal advice
- Final risk judgment
- Contract negotiation strategy
- Regulatory interpretation
- Dispute handling

## Agent-assisted work

- Contract summary
- Clause comparison
- Policy retrieval
- Obligation extraction
- Risk issue spotting

## Agent-executed work

- Intake routing
- Document metadata tagging
- Checklist validation
- Renewal reminders
- Compliance evidence collection

## Recommended agents

- Legal Intake Agent
- Contract Review Agent
- Policy Retrieval Agent
- Obligation Tracking Agent
- Compliance Evidence Agent

## Core workflows

- Contract review
- Policy review
- Compliance evidence collection
- Risk assessment
- Obligation tracking

## Memory map

- Contract memory
- Policy memory
- Risk memory
- Obligation memory
- Decision memory

## Governance

Agents do not provide final legal advice. Humans approve legal conclusions, contract positions, compliance interpretations, and external communications.

## Metrics

- Review cycle time
- Clause retrieval accuracy
- Obligation tracking completeness
- Escalation quality
- Policy freshness
- Human correction rate

## Risks

- Unauthorized legal advice
- Missed obligations
- Outdated policy retrieval
- Confidentiality breach
- Weak audit trail

## Maturity checklist

- Legal scope is explicit
- Agents cite source documents
- Final advice remains human-led
- Sensitive data access is controlled
- Compliance evidence is auditable
